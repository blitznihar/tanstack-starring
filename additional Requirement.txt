Does it show graphs and visual elements?Yes, heavily. Third graders are expected to read and interpret data using multiple visual formats. Common visuals on the test include:Bar Graphs & Pictographs: Counting and comparing items (e.g., favorite sports or drinks ordered).Dot Plots & Number Lines: Identifying fractions, measuring lengths, or tracking frequencies.Geometric Shapes: Grids showing the perimeter and area of rectangles or composite shapes.Base-10 Blocks / Fraction Strips: Shaded visual grids representing place value or equivalent fractions.📑 The 5 Question Types on STAAR MathUnder the testing framework, approximately 75% of the math test consists of multiple-choice questions, while the remaining 25% uses interactive online question styles.1. Multiple Choice (Single Choice)The classic format. The student reads a problem and clicks one correct answer out of four options (A, B, C, or D).Example: "A farmer sold 15 small pumpkins. The number of large pumpkins sold is represented by \(3 \times 15\). Which statement is true?"2. Multiselect (Multiple Choice with more than one answer)The student must choose two or three correct answers from a checkbox list. The question explicitly states how many answers to choose.Example: "Nia drew a group of shapes on a grid. Which two shapes are included in Nia's group? Select TWO correct answers."3. Equation Editor / Text Entry (Griddable)Instead of picking from options, a small calculator-style number pad pops up on the screen. The student must type the final numeric answer directly into boxes.Example: "Pat buys 4 bags of party favors. She puts 23 stickers in each bag. How many stickers does Pat need to buy altogether? Record your answer in the space provided."4. Hot SpotA graph, a map, or a set of shapes appears on the screen. The question asks the student to click directly on a specific part of the graphic (like clicking on a specific point on a number line or selecting the correct shape from a diagram).5. Drag-and-Drop / Inline ChoiceStudents use their mouse or touch screen to move numbers, symbols, or labels into blank spaces inside an equation or a table. Alternatively, they may click a drop-down menu inside a math sentence to choose the correct greater than (\(>\)), less than (\(<\)), or equal to (\(=\)) sign.🧰 Digital Tools on the Math TestWhen taking the math portion online, students will have access to unique digital tools directly on their testing screen:Digital Ruler: A movable ruler tool to measure items displayed on the screen.Graph Paper Overlay: A grid tool they can turn on to help them align figures or sketch out work.Scratchpad: A digital drawing tool allowing them to write out mathematical steps with a mouse or stylus.Would you like me to show you a mock visual problem (like an area grid or fraction number line) so your child can practice reading it, or would you like a list of core math formulas they need to know for 3rd grade?
